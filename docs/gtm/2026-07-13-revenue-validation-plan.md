# Revenue Validation Without Advertisers

**Date:** 2026-07-13
**Context:** answer to "how do I validate revenue if I can't find advertisers / how do I generate revenue at the beginning" — from the VC stress-test discussion. Complements `docs/gtm/2026-07-05-gtm-plan.md`.

---

## Part 1 — How money flows with zero advertisers

Affiliate networks exist so small publishers can monetize without any business relationship — brands have pre-agreed to pay anyone who sends a converting click.

- **Amazon Associates** — covers most retail intent. 1–4% commission. Needs a live site to approve; closes accounts without 3 sales in 180 days. Apply once real traffic exists.
- **Impact / Awin / CJ** — affiliate marketplaces (Ticketmaster, Nike, Zalando, Etsy sellers…). Apply per-program; many auto-approve small publishers.
- **Skimlinks or Sovrn Commerce** — the day-one bridge: auto-converts any merchant link into an affiliate link, ~50K merchant programs behind one signup, no per-brand applications. Lower cut, but covers nearly everything while Amazon/Impact approvals grind.
- **Travelpayouts** — travel aggregator (Booking, Kiwi, GetYourGuide); easier entry than Booking.com's tightened direct program.

The day a user says "yes, show me" and clicks, Hype can already be earning — through networks, with no sales conversations.

## Part 2 — The reframe: early revenue is not the validation

Nobody expects meaningful revenue at pre-seed. They expect proof the **ad unit** works. The revenue model is a chain of four rates:

> % of users with a relevant find moment × % who say "yes, show me" × % who click × payout per click

Affiliate links at even ~200 beta users measure the first three with real behavior; the fourth is benchmarkable from public CPC/CPA rates ($1.50–3 for consented intent, defensible vs Google Shopping) without a single advertiser deal.

The fundable slide: *"At n=300 users, 35% had ≥1 find moment/month, 45% of asks got a yes, 30% of yeses clicked — here's the cohort data."* That works even if total revenue was $47. The first commission is a build-in-public screenshot, not a revenue line anyone weighs.

## Part 3 — Validating the advertiser side without users

Parallel track, costs nothing: **10–15 customer-development calls** with D2C marketers / growth leads (small Shopify brands, event promoters, local businesses — via LinkedIn, X, indie-hacker circles). One question: *"If a user explicitly told an assistant they need X, consented to see an offer, and clicked — what's that click worth to you?"*

Three outcomes, all valuable:
1. Real willingness-to-pay numbers replace the $1.50–3.00 estimate.
2. Some say "put us in when you launch" — informal LOIs are strong pre-seed material.
3. If they all say "never above Google CPC," the premium thesis needs work — learned cheaply.

At even 1–5K engaged users, tiny **CPA-only performance deals with micro-brands** become possible well before the 10K MAU milestone — a small brand risks nothing on pay-per-conversion, so "yes" is easy. First direct revenue needs a conversation, not an ad-tech stack.

## The sequence, compressed

1. **Now:** Skimlinks/Sovrn signup (no approval wall) + file Impact/Amazon applications the day the site is live — approval time is the long pole.
2. **Beta (weeks 1–8):** measure the three rates. That IS the validation. Screenshot the first commission.
3. **In parallel:** 10 advertiser-side discovery calls → real CPC numbers + 2–3 soft LOIs.
4. **At ~1–5K users:** first CPA-only micro-brand deal — proves brands will deal directly.
5. **Pitch with:** three measured rates + benchmark CPCs + LOIs = a revenue model *with evidence*, which at pre-seed beats revenue.

**One-line answer:** you don't validate revenue by earning it — you validate it by measuring the yes-rate and click-rate on real users, and letting affiliate networks (no relationship required) pay out whatever small amounts prove the loop closes end to end.
